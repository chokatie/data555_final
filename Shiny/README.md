## Research Topic: *Severe Maternal Morbidity in the US (2016-2022)* <br>
 > In the United States, severe maternal morbidity (SMM) affects more than 60,000 women annually. Frequently described as a “maternal near-miss,” SMM refers to unexpected, life-threatening complications that occur during pregnancy, childbirth, or the postpartum period. Despite its high prevalence, one-third to two-thirds of all SMM cases are deemed preventable and linked to failures in clinical decision-making, care processes, and health system performance. This poses serious concerns, as women who encounter SMM are at elevated risk of experiencing postpartum depression and anxiety, adverse perinatal outcomes, and chronic conditions.
        

#### Importance <br>
 > Severe maternal morbidity is an important public health outcome as it reflects serious pregnancy-related complications and can reveal inequities in maternal care. This dashboard helps identify racial disparities and time trends that may inform maternal health policy, equity-focused interventions, and quality improvement efforts.
